Finchy's Advanced Guide to Scientific Primitive Material Operations

This is an in depth explanation of Tinker's Construct (or TCon) tools and effective utilization of them.

Chapter 0: Words and Vocabulary --some of these won't make sense before you've read the rest

	Blocks and Stations
Part Builder: Block used to make parts out of materials
Pattern Table: Block used to make patterns for the Part Builder	Tool Station: Block used to make tools out of parts, as well as add modifiers and replace parts
Pattern Chest: A block that stores patterns and casts for convenience.

	Tools and Tool Stats
Tool: Item that breaks blocks or hurts enemies
Modifier: Some effect that the tool has
Material: The thing that a tool part is made of
Material Modifier: A modifier that comes with the material
Attack Damage: The damage that a tool deals, or a part provides innately
Mining Speed: The speed with which a tool breaks blocks
Mining Level: Determines what a pickaxe or shovel can mine
Durability: How long a tool can be used before breaking, and needing to be repaired
Effective Durability: How long a tool can be used, taking into account the chance for durability to not be consumed
Modifier Slots: The capacity for a tool to accept modifiers
Mining XP: The capacity for a tool to increase its mining level

	Smelting and Heavy Smithing
Smeltery: Large structure that processes molten metals
Pattern: Used to craft a pattern for use in a Part Builder
Cast: Used to cast tool parts in a Smeltery
Extruder: Electric machine that can produce tool parts oof advanced materials
Fluid solidifer: Electric machine that can produce crossbow bolt casts of advanced materials
	
Chapter 1: Tools, and making them.

	1.1: Why use TCon?
	A TCon tool is a big undertaking relative to simply making an iron pickaxe, but with a little bit of care and attention, can be much more powerful. The tools are generally stronger, faster, and will never break permanently. They can be modified and improved without ever having to craft a new one, allowing your tools to be permanent over the course of an entire game. They're also much cheaper, both in the amount of materials used to affect the world, and in the cost to actually make and improve them.
	
	1.2: Tools and using them
	A tool is a general word for 1 of 5 general kinds of thing that you can make in a Tool Station. A tool can be, broadly: A simple tool, that only breaks one block at a time; A heavy tool, that breaks either a 3x3 or a 3x3x3 of blocks at a time; A melee weapon, that hits enemies and does a bunch of damage; A ranged weapon, that hits enemies from far away and does a bunch of damage; Finally, ammunition, which is generally consumed by a ranged weapon. These are the main things you want to use from TCon, so they're what this guide is going to focus on.
	When a tools durability hits 0, the tool is broken, and needs to be repaired. There is not cost for repairing other than the material used to do so. Ammunition has durability in the form of ammo count, and is replenished in the same way as a tools durability.
